Overview:

The Standards and Testing Agency (STA) is administering the multiplication tables check (MTC) information communications technology (ICT) survey. The survey will involve a sample of schools in England, and be undertaken over the period September - October 2018.

STA will use the results of the survey to:

  • understand the types and capacity of devices and systems/infrastructure in use in schools
  • develop and refine the digital service for implementing live checks

STA is the data controller for the survey and is responsible for determining how personal data collected during the period will be treated. We will not publish the results of the survey, and no individual school will be identified.

With regards to data processing, STA is responsible for securely storing and handling any personal data required to administer the survey. STA will securely dispose of this data in line with the requirements of UK data protection legislation and the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) .

What is the legal basis for collecting and processing the data?

The legal basis for processing the personal data used and collected during the 2018 MTC trial is covered by:

  • _Data Protection Act 2018, Part 2 Chapter 2 Lawfulness of processing Section 8 (d), which allows for when (data) processing is necessary for the exercise of a function of the Crown, a Minister of the Crown or a government department.
  • _GDPR Article 6 (1) (e), which states that the processing is for the performance of a task carried out in the public interest, or in the exercise of the data controller’s official authority, where the processing is necessary for the exercise of a function of the Crown, a Minister of the Crown or a government department and is a proportionate means of achieving a legitimate aim. This provision covers personal data.

What data will be collected and who can access it?

STA will collect and hold school ICT infrastructure and system data and information gathered from the survey. This will include:

  • School internet browser settings
  • School internet connection speed
  • ICT device type (if available)
  • School ICT operating system
  • ICT device screen width
  • Any additional data or information provided on the online feedback form

The MTC ICT survey is to be completed online by a selected sample of schools.

Access to the survey results will be restricted to those STA employees who need to see the survey data to inform the final design and development of the live MTC services. All data for the survey will be supplied in electronic data files and sent by secure file transfer protocol (SFTP).

No information or data collected during the trial will be transferred or stored outside of the European Economic Area (EEA).

STA handles personal data in accordance with the rights given to individuals under UK data protection legislation. Data subjects have a right to see information held about them and STA will cooperate fully when a subject access request is made.

Retaining personal data

Data collected by STA will be retained for up to 6 months following the end of the survey, STA will destroy data and permanently delete computer files from their systems.

STA will only keep personal data for as long as it is needed for the purposes of this project, after which point it will be securely destroyed. Please note that, under Article 5(1)(e) GDPR 2018, and in compliance with the relevant conditions, we can lawfully keep personal data processed purely for research purposes indefinitely.

Further information

If you have any queries about MTC trial data, please email MTC.STA@education.gov.uk.

STA, as an executive agency of the Department for Education (DfE), determines the purposes and means of processing personal data as part of this project. You can find more information in the DfE’s personal information charter .

You have the right to lodge a complaint about data protection issues with the Information Commissioner’s Office. More information is available on the website .
